# Aequor Intelligence

Aequor Intelligence is a prototype decision support platform designed to improve transparency and analytical awareness within the maritime fuel and port infrastructure ecosystem.

The system integrates structured market data, port level intelligence, and analytical visualization tools into a unified terminal style interface. The objective of the platform is to allow users to monitor port readiness for emerging marine fuels, evaluate supply conditions, and interpret price signals relevant to maritime fuel procurement and logistics.

The project explores how fragmented maritime information sources can be consolidated into a coherent analytical environment for infrastructure and fuel market analysis.

---

## Project Motivation

The motivation for the project emerged from an interest in the structural transformation currently affecting the shipping industry.

Maritime transport is entering a period of fuel transition where traditional bunker fuels are gradually being complemented by alternative energy carriers such as e-methanol and ammonia. At the same time, information related to port infrastructure readiness, fuel availability, supplier presence, and price indicators is distributed across many separate data sources.

Aequor Intelligence was developed as an experimental attempt to consolidate these signals into a single analytical interface that allows users to interpret the broader structure of maritime fuel markets.

---

## Platform Overview

The system is organized as a terminal style analytical environment similar in structure to interfaces used in financial or commodity markets. The platform integrates several modules that together allow users to explore port infrastructure conditions, fuel market indicators, and operational updates relevant to maritime fuel procurement.

---

## Core System Modules

### Port Intelligence Terminal

The Port Intelligence Terminal aggregates structured information about individual ports. The interface displays indicators related to fuel availability, supplier presence, infrastructure readiness, and indicative price ranges. Users can compare ports based on operational characteristics such as delivery time, supplier participation, and confidence indicators associated with infrastructure readiness.

### Market Intelligence Screener

The Market Intelligence Screener allows users to filter and compare ports based on operational parameters. Ports can be analyzed according to fuel availability, number of suppliers, confidence scores, and price ranges. This module supports the identification of potential bunkering locations and regional supply conditions.

### Market Indicators Terminal

The Indicators Terminal monitors benchmark indices that provide context for maritime fuel and freight markets. These include freight market benchmarks such as Baltic freight indices, bunker price references, and broader commodity indicators including crude oil and natural gas benchmarks. These signals help users interpret price dynamics affecting marine fuel procurement.

### Port Intelligence Feed

The Port Intelligence Feed aggregates operational updates associated with specific ports. These updates may include infrastructure announcements, regulatory developments, certification updates, or supply related events. The feed is designed to help users follow developments that may influence port readiness for alternative fuels.

### Analytical Visualization Tools

The system includes visualization tools designed to support exploratory analysis. These tools include price history charts, comparative terminal views, and dashboards that display supplier availability, fuel price ranges, and infrastructure readiness indicators.

---

## System Concept

The central concept behind Aequor Intelligence is the organization of fragmented maritime information streams into a structured analytical interface.

Information relevant to maritime fuel procurement often exists across multiple news sources, infrastructure reports, databases, and operational updates. By consolidating these signals within a single interface, the platform attempts to reduce information friction and improve analytical awareness.

The system structure resembles a financial information terminal in which ports function as analytical nodes, fuel markets provide pricing signals, and operational updates act as event driven information streams.

While the current prototype operates with demonstration data rather than live feeds, the architecture illustrates how such a system could eventually integrate automated data collection and real time market signals.

## Industry Dialogue

The concept of Aequor Intelligence has been shared with **Green Marine**, a maritime infrastructure and fuel services company, for preliminary review.

The purpose of this dialogue is to explore whether the platform could support internal analysis of maritime fuel markets and infrastructure readiness.

At the time of writing, the system is under evaluation and no formal agreement has been established.

---

## Future Development

The current version represents a conceptual demonstration of how maritime intelligence software could be structured.

Future development could include:

- automated data collection pipelines  
- real time market feeds  
- expanded port infrastructure databases  
- analytical tools for evaluating alternative fuel supply networks  

Such developments could allow the platform to support more comprehensive analysis of maritime fuel procurement and infrastructure readiness within the shipping sector.
